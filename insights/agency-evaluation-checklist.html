The 15-Point Agency Audit

Cut through the noise and hire for outcomes.

Choosing a digital marketing agency in India often feels like a gamble. You’re met with smooth-talking account managers, flashy "vanity metric" dashboards, and the constant promise of "going viral." But for a B2B SaaS founder with a ₹2–5L monthly budget, "viral" doesn't pay the bills—pipeline does. The industry is currently plagued by "Black Box" agencies that hide their process behind jargon and junior-level execution. You aren't just hiring a vendor; you are hiring a temporary extension of your growth team. If they can’t show you exactly how the sausage is made, who is actually doing the work, and how they link their activities to your bottom-line revenue, you aren't buying a service—you're buying a liability. This 15-point checklist is designed to cut through the fluff, helping you identify agencies that lead with senior-level practitioners, verifiable social proof, and a roadmap built on business outcomes, not just clicks.

The Trust Deficit: Why Most Agency Relationships Fail

The core problem isn't a lack of talent; it's a lack of transparency. Many agencies in India operate on a high-volume, low-touch model where the senior expert who sold you the contract disappears on day one, replaced by a junior account manager with zero strategic context.

Did you know?

A recent study showed that 60% of B2B clients felt "ghosted" by their agency once the contract was signed. This is the "Service Gap" that destroys ROI.

The 15-Point Evaluation Framework

1
Senior Practitioner Access

Will you have direct access to the strategy lead, or just an account manager? At Vedisha, practitioners lead every call.

2
Verifiable Social Proof

Can they provide LinkedIn profiles of the team actually doing the work? Anonymous "Creative Lead" roles are a major red flag.

3
Real-Time Transparency

Do you get a 24/7 dashboard or just a PDF report at the end of the month? If it's the latter, they are hiding the data.

4
ICP Clarity

Can they define your Ideal Customer Profile within 48 hours? If they can't, they'll default to low-intent, generic traffic.

5-15
Rigor & Results

From technical SEO health to creative quality assurance, ensure every step is documented and linked to your bottom line.

Agencies that can't define your ICP within the first 48 hours usually default to generic, low-intent traffic. Data shows that 90% of "guaranteed ranking" schemes use risky PBNs that result in long-term domain penalties.
